About the Rabies Virus

A virus is an infectious agent that replicates itself in the host.A virus can only replicate itself in other living organisms. When it is in the human body, it triggers an immune response that most of the times eliminates the virus. Some viruses evade these responses and cause chronic infections. Rabies causes inflammation in the host’s brain. Symptoms include fever and tingling at the site of exposure.
